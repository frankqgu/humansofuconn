Honore

I can start from middle school, where the teachers were telling us to dream big, even one day, they came into class to give a whole father talk to us like how it is important to dream big. That was in Africa, Rwanda.

After I head to the states, the teachers help me to learn the language, the culture. When I got to college, I met a great counselor. When I was behind, he got me back on track. He helped me find those connections I don’t think I could’ve on my own.

But going to a public school in Hartford, There were some resources we didn't have at the school that private school students had. The government really cares so that people have a voice in certain zip codes. So it doesn't make me feel that we still have a long way to go cause the government should be treating each student the same.

I'll say there is more hope for education because if you see where education started is not where it is right now, there were some people who were not allowed to be in the schools that can come. So my kids will see. Oh, my dad went today. So I have the right to be here. I don't have to be ashamed of anything.
